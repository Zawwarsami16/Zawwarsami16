# WHO

Independent engineer. Canada.

I build reasoning systems for operators. The kind of tools that compress what a research desk does into something a single person can run. Most of my work is private. The public surface is the half I am willing to put a name on.

---

## What I do

- **AI engineering.** Agents, tool use, reasoning systems, memory and compaction. I work daily with the Anthropic SDKs, vercel/ai, and ollama at the lib-code level, not just the API surface.
- **Offensive security.** HTB, CTFs, vulnerability research. I read source before I run fuzzers. The patterns folder grows faster than the writeups folder. That is on purpose.
- **Markets and macro.** Anteroom Oracle, the AI terminal that compresses macro and geopolitical signal into a single operator's terminal.
- **Long-form writing.** *Unwrapping What Exists* is the book in progress. Substack collects the smaller pieces I find on the way.

## How I work

- I read the source before I fuzz the API. Source review is a superset of every black-box technique you can run against the artifact.
- I write down the pattern the first time I see it. The first time costs twenty minutes. The second time costs two minutes. After that the file in `patterns/` costs nothing.
- I keep the public surface tight. Polished things ship. Drafts stay drafts. There is no value in shipping a draft.
- I run one studio, not five disconnected side projects. Everything I build is supposed to compound with everything else I build.
- I do not pad commits to keep the contribution graph green. If a day's real work is below the floor, I find more real work; I do not fake it.
